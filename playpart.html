Title:An Enemy of the People

author:Henrik Ibsen

cloud
1st Citizen (meeting another)

. Hullo, Lamstad! You here too?

cloud
2nd Citizen.

I go to every public meeting, I do.

cloud
3rd Citizen.

Brought your whistle too, I expect!

cloud
2nd Citizen.

I should think so. Haven't you?

cloud
3rd Citizen.

Rather! And old Evensen said he was going to bring a cow-horn, he did.

cloud
2nd Citizen.

Good old Evensen! (Laughter among the crowd.)

cloud
4th Citizen

(coming up to them). I say, tell me what is going on here tonight?

cloud
2nd Citizen.

Dr. Stockmann is going to deliver an address attacking the Mayor.

cloud
4th Citizen.

But the Mayor is his brother.

cloud
1st Citizen.

That doesn't matter; Dr. Stockmann's not the chap to be afraid.

cloud
3rd Citizen.

But he is in the wrong; it said so in the “People’s Messenger.”

cloud
2nd Citizen.

Yes, I expect he must be in the wrong this time, because neither the Householders’ Association nor the Citizens’ Club would lend him their hall for his meeting.

cloud
1st Citizen.

He couldn’t even get the loan of the hall at the Baths.

cloud
2nd Citizen.

No, I should think not.A Man in another part of the crowd. I say—who are we to back up in this? Another Man, beside him. Watch Aslaksen, and do as he does Billing (pushing his way through the crowd, with a writing-case under his arm). Excuse me, gentlemen—do you mind letting me through? I am reporting for the “People’s Messenger.” Thank you very much! (He sits down at the table on the left.)
